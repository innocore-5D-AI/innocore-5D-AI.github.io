## Our Mission

The **5D AI Robotics Initiative (5D AI-RI)** is a KAIST-led InnoCORE research program dedicated to developing next-generation autonomous robots capable of understanding and interacting with the physical world through advanced 5D world models.

Our mission is to cultivate top-tier AI robotics talent in Korea while advancing cutting-edge research that bridges the gap between technology demonstrations and real-world applications.

## Why 5D AI?

Current Physical AI technologies face significant challenges:

- **Complex Process Vulnerability**: Efficiency drops sharply when multiple processes or situations are combined
- **Domain Limitations**: Applications largely limited to automotive assembly and logistics
- **Productivity Gap**: Current robots achieve only 30-50% of human worker productivity
- **Long-tail Problem**: Achieving 100% human-level performance may take 10+ years

Our 5D approach addresses these challenges by going beyond traditional 3D representations to include **temporal dynamics** and **physical properties** (material, mass, friction, tactile response), enabling robots to truly understand and predict real-world interactions.

## Research Focus

### Division 1: Adaptive Robot Platform
- Development of adaptive actuators and high-resolution tactile sensors
- Human-level precision interaction interfaces
- Real-time reflex control systems with distributed intelligence
- Multi-robot platforms with embedded intelligence modules

### Division 2: 5D Robot AI
- 5D World Foundation Model using scene graphs
- Whole-body manipulation foundation models for diverse robots
- Real-time robot response prediction systems
- Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINN) for world modeling
